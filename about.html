About Vishasita

The Indus Valley Civilization is perhaps the last major, early civilization whose script is undeciphered. Although it is now close to a century since the early excavations of Harappa and Mohenjo-Daro, there have been few attempts amd none of them are close to getting widespread acceptance.

The necessary information and resources for an attempt at decipherment have not been available in the public domain. This limits the problem to a handful of researchers. We believe that it is important to open them up to the brilliance and genius of all mankind. We are focussed on creating quality resources and releasing them in a free and open manner to the web.

We are using Archeological Survey of India (ASI) Reports from the original digs as the basis to hand create all the resources offered at Vishasita. These reports are more than 75 years since their first publication and we believe that the photos of seals in them are now in public domain. The resources that we have created like our eConcordance or our font were built manually from these photos allowing us to offer them in a transparent, free and open license.

Goals

We wish to make available a representative sample of information on the Harappan Script in a free and open manner to the public. This includes the photos of original seals that have gone out of copyright as well as useful resources like a Harappan font which allows efficient sharing online; an electronic corpus of seal text to allow for machine learning and other algorithmic analysis; as well as a seal image search engine so that people can efficiently navigate what is out there.

We wish to help build engagement in this domain by increasing awareness of the various aspects of what would be required in a decipherment. We hope to create and share a decipherment ourselves. It is an explicit goal to share the technical challenges and limitations in our approach transparently so that others can leverage our experience.

We would like to increase awareness of this great civilization among the public and build a community around this topic. We realize that it is currently the silent heritage of millions of people and we wish to do so in manner that is respectful of that importance.

Non-Goals

We do not wish to prescribe technical solutions. As an example, in our open font we needed to assign a temporary encoding and get a rough and ready solution. Although we hope that our efforts can inform a future attempt at a standardized encoding for the language, our users should not expect that final solution will resemble the one that we are using today.

We do not wish to be a general repository of public domain resources in this field. While we would encourage all parties who have resources relevant to decipherment to release them in the public domain, we do not expect them to do so in Vishasita. We lack the legal resources required for such an effort and the technical solutions required do not need to depend on anything we do.

We are not trying to champion a single solution or decipherment. We believe that like science, there will be many theories and the most elegant ones, ones which require the least leap of faith while explaining the maximum amounts of facts, are more likely to be correct. At least until a better one comes along. We see our role as facilitator in that process rather than an activist or critic.
